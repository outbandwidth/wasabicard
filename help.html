Frequently Asked Questions

How do I activate my physical Wasabi Card?

To activate your physical Wasabi Card, log in to your mobile app or web account, navigate to the Cards section, and select "Activate Card." You'll need to enter the last 4 digits of your card number and the CVV. Alternatively, you can call our automated activation line at the number printed on the sticker on your new card.

What are the fees for using Wasabi Card?

Wasabi Card offers a transparent fee structure with no hidden charges. Basic accounts have no monthly fees, and most domestic transfers are free. For international transfers, we charge a small fee (typically 0.5-1%) which is lower than most banks. ATM withdrawals are free up to certain limits, after which a 2% fee applies. Please refer to our Fees page for a comprehensive breakdown of all charges.

How long do transfers take to process?

Transfers between Wasabi Card accounts are instant. Domestic bank transfers typically take 1-2 business days to process. International transfers can take 3-5 business days depending on the destination country and banking system. Please note that transfers initiated after business hours or on weekends/holidays will begin processing on the next business day.

What should I do if I lose my card?

If you lose your Wasabi Card, immediately freeze it through the mobile app or web dashboard to prevent unauthorized use. Navigate to the Cards section, select the lost card, and tap "Freeze Card." Then report the loss by contacting our support team. We'll help you order a replacement card. Remember to update any recurring payments once you receive your new card.

How does Wasabi Card protect my data and money?

Wasabi Card employs bank-level encryption for all transactions and data storage. We use two-factor authentication, biometric verification, and real-time fraud monitoring systems to protect your account. All funds are held in regulated financial institutions and are protected by appropriate deposit insurance (up to applicable limits). Additionally, our zero-liability policy protects you from unauthorized transactions if reported promptly.
